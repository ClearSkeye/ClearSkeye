# ClearSkeye Brand and Design System

> Sight before design.

**Version 1.0** • Owner: Darren, ClearSkeye

This is the canonical machine readable mirror of the ClearSkeye brand and design system. It is the file artificial intelligence assistants (Claude, Cursor) and humans who prefer plain text use as the source of truth. The Word document held alongside this file is its visual twin and the file used by external agencies. If the two ever conflict, this file is the authority.

The system is organised in six parts: brand foundation, verbal identity, visual identity, digital system, working with artificial intelligence, governance.

---

## Part one. Brand foundation

### How to use this document

Three audiences share the same standard. Design and creative agencies producing work on the firm's behalf. Internal staff and contractors writing or designing in the firm's voice. Software tools, including artificial intelligence assistants, generating copy, code, or visual artefacts that carry the firm's name.

Where this document is silent, the conviction stated below decides the answer. ClearSkeye sees before it designs. If a creative or technical decision contradicts that conviction, the conviction wins.

### The conviction

Most operating models are designed before they are understood. ClearSkeye reverses the order.

This sentence is the keel of the brand. Every page of marketing, every proposal, every closing artefact, every team conversation, every hire is traceable to it. The conviction names the failure mode of the consulting industry without naming any competitor, and it positions the practice as the firm that resists the rush to design.

### The promise

You will know what your operating model is for before we change a single line of it.

This is the promise made to a chief executive on day one of an engagement. It commits the firm to sequencing purpose ahead of process.

### The tagline

**Sight before design.**

Three words. One full stop. Always sentence case with a final full stop. Never set in capitals. Never broken across lines. Used singularly, never as part of a longer sentence.

### The audience

ClearSkeye is built for the most senior executives at Fortune 500 enterprises. The buyer titles expected most often are chief executive officer, chief operating officer, chief financial officer, chief transformation officer, and group head of strategy. The supporting reader at the next layer down is typically a senior vice president of operations or a programme director responsible for a multi-year operating model change.

These buyers reward firms that say less and mean more. The brand earns trust at this level by withholding rather than by reaching. Restraint is the luxury signal.

### The personality

Five steady positions on the dimensions where consultancies most often miscalibrate.

- Confident, not loud.
- Considered, not slow.
- Precise, not technical.
- Warm, not casual.
- Senior, not stiff.

### Brand architecture

ClearSkeye is a single master brand. The firm itself is the only branded entity. Two named assets are exceptions.

- **The Method**, the four word engagement sequence: **Purpose. Sight. Design. Practice.** Treated as a named asset, initial capitals on each word.
- **A Clear View**, the named closing artefact delivered at the end of every engagement. Always written in full, initial capitals, in roman type.

All other engagement components, deliverables, and internal processes are described descriptively and never receive a brand name.

### The method, made visible

**Purpose. Sight. Design. Practice.**

- **Purpose.** Articulating the why. We will not redesign a structure we cannot explain the purpose of.
- **Sight.** Patient observation of the operating model as it actually is. We replace assumed reality with observed reality.
- **Design.** The future state across structure, spans, layers, centralisation choices, process, and technology.
- **Practice.** Embedding, governance, control and measurement. The discipline that keeps the design alive after we leave.

The sequence is the practice's signature. It appears on the website footer, on every proposal cover, and on the inside front page of every closing document. Never abbreviated. Never reordered.

## Part two. Verbal identity

### Voice principles

In order of priority. When two principles seem to conflict, the higher one wins.

1. **Specificity over abstraction.** Name the thing. If you could substitute a different industry without changing the meaning, the sentence is too abstract.
2. **Restraint over emphasis.** Do not stack adjectives. Do not bold or italicise inside body copy. Use position and white space to give a sentence weight.
3. **Conviction over hedge.** Do not write "we believe" or "we think" before our own assertions. Where we genuinely do not know, say so plainly.
4. **Plain words over jargon.** If a word would not be used at the dinner table by an educated reader, choose another. The exception is operating model, which is the technical term for the thing we design.
5. **Sentences over slogans.** The brand has one tagline. Beyond that, sentences.

### Tone calibration

Voice does not change. Tone shifts by surface.

- **Most formal** (regulator, board paper, contract): clinical, no metaphor, no tagline use.
- **Standard** (website, proposal, A Clear View): the default register. Considered, plain, occasionally observational. The tagline is appropriate.
- **Lighter** (thank you note, internal, conference write-up): slightly warmer, slightly shorter. Still no slang, no exclamation marks.

### Vocabulary

**Words we use:** see, look, observe, watch, notice, sight, observation, clarity, perspective, design, structure, spans, layers, federation, centralisation, process, procedure, control, measurement, purpose, the work, the discipline, the practice, the method, the engagement, the closing document, considered, deliberate, faithful, accurate, patient, exact.

**Words we do not use:** leverage, synergy, transformation (as a noun naming the firm or its offer), deliver value, drive impact, holistic, world class, best in class, cutting edge, paradigm, unlock, ideate, deep dive, low hanging fruit, move the needle, north star, in the DNA of, journey (as a metaphor), pivot, accelerate, supercharge, game changer, end to end (as a marketing claim), bespoke (overused), tailored, results driven.

**Acronyms:** avoid where possible. The firm spells out chief executive officer, chief operating officer, target operating model, and similar terms on every public surface. Internally, abbreviated forms are acceptable in working documents only.

### Sentence and paragraph patterns

Three patterns recur.

- **The two beat sentence.** A claim, a full stop, the qualification. "We design operating models. We begin with purpose."
- **The three beat paragraph.** Three short sentences. The first observes. The second names. The third commits.
- **The named noun.** When introducing a method or artefact, name it once and use the name afterwards.

### Headlines, body, captions

- **Headlines.** Sentence case. End with a full stop where the headline is a complete sentence. Never exceed twelve words. The strongest are six to ten.
- **Body copy.** Editorial serif at body size. Justified left, ragged right. Generous leading. No widow or orphan single words. No bold for emphasis inside a paragraph.
- **Captions and small text.** Supporting sans serif at small size. One sentence where possible.

### Capitalisation, punctuation, dates and numbers

- Sentence case for headlines, subheadings, navigation, buttons, and form labels. Title case is reserved for the firm name and the named assets.
- Oxford comma is mandatory in serial lists.
- **No em dash. No en dash.** Use commas, semicolons, full stops, or restructure.
- Single space between sentences.
- Smart quotes (curly), never straight, in all client facing copy.
- Dates written 7 May 2026, never 07/05/2026 and never May 7th.
- Numbers under ten written as words. Numbers ten and above in figures. Exceptions for percentages, currency, and measurements.
- Currency written with the symbol and no space, for example $50 million.

### Worked examples (approved as written)

**About the work:**

**About the founder:**

**About the method:**

## Part three. Visual identity

### Logo and wordmark

The ClearSkeye logo is a wordmark. The name is set in the brand display serif at light weight, with a single graphic gesture: the descender of the Y in Skeye is extended into a vertical line that drops below the baseline by approximately one cap height. This descender is the sightline.

**Construction.** Wordmark set as a single line. Y descender is one stroke unit thick, matching the thinnest stroke of the serif at the chosen size. Descender ends with a square cap, not a serif. Descender length is fixed in proportion to the cap height of the wordmark.

**Clear space.** Minimum clear space around the wordmark equals the cap height of the C in ClearSkeye. In premium contexts (covers, hero spaces, the last page of A Clear View) the clear space is doubled.

**Minimum size.** Never smaller than 18 millimetres wide in print, or 96 pixels wide on screen.

**Permitted colour applications:**
- Ink on Paper (default).
- Ink on White (acceptable for documents and digital surfaces where Paper is unavailable).
- Paper on Ink (inverted, for dark covers and full bleed photography).
- Ink with the sightline descender in Horizon. Reserved for the cover of A Clear View, the firm's annual review, and the homepage hero only.

**Misuse:**
- Do not stretch, condense, rotate, skew, or outline the wordmark.
- Do not change the typeface or the weight.
- Do not place the wordmark over busy imagery.
- Do not pair the wordmark with another logo on the same line. Co-branding is permitted only where the second logo sits beneath a hairline rule, beneath the ClearSkeye wordmark, and at no greater visual weight.
- Do not animate the wordmark beyond a single, slow reveal of the sightline descender.

### The sightline mark

Where the wordmark cannot be used (favicon, app icon, social profile thumbnail, embroidery, foil stamp at small size) a single graphic mark is used in its place. A vertical line, one stroke unit wide, intersected at one third from the top by a horizontal line of equal stroke. The horizontal extends to either side of the vertical by an equal distance. The mark reads as a sightline crossing a horizon, or as a quiet plus sign with intent.

The sightline mark is never used alongside the wordmark. They are alternatives, not companions.

### Typography

**Primary system.** Both typefaces licensed from Klim Type Foundry.

- **Display serif:** Tiempos Headline. Headlines, the wordmark, single-sentence editorial moments. Weights: Light, Regular, Medium.
- **Text sans serif:** Söhne. Body, navigation, captions, forms, all interface type. Weights: Buch (regular), Kräftig (semibold), Halbfett (bold) for the rare case where bold is justified.

**Open source alternatives** (calibrated stand-ins, used on rapid prototypes and platforms that cannot embed the licensed fonts):
- Display alternative: Spectral, weights 300 and 400 (Google Fonts).
- Text alternative: Inter, weights 400 and 500 (Google Fonts).

**Word-safe stand-ins** (internal Word documents only):
- Display: Georgia.
- Text: Calibri.

**Setting rules:**
- Display sizes set in Tiempos Headline Light. Headings 1 to 3 set in Regular.
- Body, navigation, captions and interface type set in Söhne Buch.
- Letter spacing on display sizes reduced 1 to 2 percent. Letter spacing on small caps and eyebrows increased 8 to 12 percent.
- Long form reading width constrained to 65 to 75 characters per line.
- Hyphenation enabled on long form, disabled on short copy and headlines.

### Layout and grid

- Twelve column grid for desktop pages.
- Maximum content width 1280 pixels. Reading column maximum 720 pixels for long form.
- Gutters: 24 pixels mobile, 32 pixels tablet, 48 pixels desktop.
- Page margins: 24 pixels mobile, 48 pixels tablet, 96 pixels minimum on desktop. Hero pages may use 128 pixels.
- Spacing follows an 8 point base. Tokens: 4, 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128. Use multiples of these and nothing else.

White space is a primary brand asset. Pages should feel under-filled by industry standards.

### Imagery

**Permitted: long horizon.** Still, wide format photography of horizons. Sea horizons, alpine ridge lines, prairie skies, distant cityscape silhouettes against open sky. No people in foreground. No motion. Soft, diffused light. Used at full bleed or as full-width section breaks. Used rarely.

**Permitted: long form portrait.** Portraits of named individuals at the firm or named clients. Neutral grounds. Considered, slightly low key lighting. The subject looks at the camera or at a thoughtful middle distance. No power-pose framing. No conference room backgrounds.

**Forbidden:**
- Stock photography of any kind.
- Boardroom scenes, handshake images, generic office life.
- People pointing at screens, charts on glass walls, sticky notes on whiteboards.
- Composite illustration suggesting collaboration or innovation.
- Diverse hands-on-table images in any form.
- Cityscape skylines used decoratively.

### Iconography

Single, sparing system. Line icons only, drawn at one stroke unit thickness. No fills. Square caps. Rounded corners no greater than 1 pixel. Twenty-four pixel grid. Designed in line with the sightline mark.

If a third party icon library is used as the base (Phosphor, Lucide), only the thin or regular line variants are permitted, and only after recolouring to Ink or Sightline.

### Data visualisation

- One colour per chart by default. Ink on Paper. Where a second colour is needed, Horizon. A third colour requires a justified case.
- Gridlines in Rule colour, only where required. Often unnecessary.
- No three dimensional charts under any circumstances.
- Axis labels and units in Söhne Buch at small size, in Sightline.
- Annotations placed inline with the data, not in separate legend blocks where avoidable.
- Chart titles in Söhne Kräftig at body size, in Ink. Sources in small Sightline below.

### Document templates

Five recurring formats. Each has a master template held in the asset library.

- **Letterhead.** A4 portrait. Wordmark in Ink, top left, 32 millimetres wide, with cap height clear space below. Body in Calibri 11 point at 1.5 line spacing. Footer with firm name, registered address, and company number in Sightline at 8 point. Margins: 32 mm top and left, 24 mm right and bottom.
- **Business card.** 85 by 55 millimetres. Front: wordmark centred, Ink on Paper. Reverse: name in Tiempos Regular 11 point, role in Söhne Buch 9 point Sightline, contact details in Söhne Buch 8 point Ink, all left aligned, with the tagline set bottom right in Söhne Buch 7 point Sightline. Stock: 350gsm uncoated, soft white. No foil. No spot finishes.
- **Proposal cover.** A4 portrait. Cover hierarchy from top: ClearSkeye wordmark, eyebrow line stating proposal type, headline naming the client and engagement, single horizon image (optional), method line at the foot, date in Sightline below.
- **A Clear View.** A4 portrait, 15 to 20 pages. Cover: wordmark with sightline descender in Horizon, the title "A Clear View" in Tiempos Light at display-2, client name in Tiempos Regular at heading-1, date in Sightline. Inside front: the conviction sentence and the method line. Body: editorial layout with one column reading width and generous margins. Closing page: the four word method, the signature of the engagement lead, the date.
- **Slide deck.** 16 by 9 ratio. Paper background. Body in Söhne Buch at 18 to 22 points. Section opens use a single Tiempos Light line on Paper with a hairline rule in Horizon below. Maximum five lines of text per slide. No bullet characters: indent or use a hairline rule. Speaker notes carry the detail.

## Part four. Digital system

### Web design principles

1. **Quiet by default.** The page is mostly empty. Type and a single mark do the work.
2. **Reading is the primary mode.** Designed for someone who will spend three minutes reading, not three seconds skimming.
3. **One idea per surface.** A page argues for one position.
4. **Never bury the conviction.** It appears in some form on every primary page, including the homepage above the fold.
5. **Performance is part of the brand.** Site loads in under two seconds on a 4G connection.

### Component patterns

**Buttons and links:**
- Primary action: Söhne Kräftig, Paper on Ink, 24 pixel vertical padding, 32 pixel horizontal padding, 4 pixel corner radius. One per page.
- Secondary action: Söhne Buch in Ink, with a 1 pixel Ink underline. No background, no border.
- Inline link: Ink with a 1 pixel underline, offset 4 pixels below the baseline.
- Hover: underline thickens to 2 pixels. No colour change. No motion beyond the underline.

**Forms:**
- Single column. One field per row.
- Labels above fields, Söhne Buch small size, Sightline.
- Field borders 1 pixel in Rule colour. On focus, border becomes Ink at 2 pixels. No coloured glow.
- Errors set in Halt, with the explanation as a full sentence below the field.

**Cards and sections:**
- Cards have no shadow by default. Boundary is a hairline rule in Rule colour.
- Section breaks are achieved with white space, not with background colour blocks.
- Where a coloured block is necessary, Paper Light or Meridian. Never Horizon for full sections.

### Motion

A single behaviour: a slow fade or rise of 8 to 16 pixels over 400 to 600 milliseconds, on first reveal only. No infinite loops. No parallax. No bouncing. No carousels.

A user who arrives expecting calm is calmed by stillness, not by elegance of animation.

Always honour `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### What artificial intelligence is not allowed to generate

These artefacts are produced only with human authorship and approval, regardless of how strong a draft an assistant produces.

- The conviction sentence, the promise sentence, and the tagline.
- The four word method.
- Any copy that goes on the homepage above the fold.
- Any copy that goes inside A Clear View.
- Any copy that names a specific client without that client's written approval.
- The logo, the wordmark, or any variant of the sightline mark.
- Imagery of named individuals.

### Reviewing artificial intelligence output

Every artefact produced by an assistant is reviewed by a human contributor against the voice rules and the mechanical rules in this document before it leaves the firm.

## Part six. Governance and stewardship

### Ownership

The brand is owned by the founder. Material decisions sit with the owner. Day to day stewardship is delegated to a brand custodian (a named individual at the firm or at a retained agency) who maintains the asset library, the rules files, and the version history of this document.

### Asset library

All canonical assets live in a single location, available to staff and approved contractors. Inventory:

- This document, in current and historic versions, in Word and markdown.
- The CSS design tokens file and the Tailwind preset file.
- Logo files in vector format (SVG, EPS) and raster (PNG at multiple sizes).
- Sightline mark files in the same formats.
- Font files (or licence references) for Tiempos Headline and Söhne, plus the open source alternatives.
- Word, PowerPoint, and Keynote templates.
- A Clear View master template.
- Letterhead, business card, and proposal cover templates.
- Approved imagery, with usage rights documented.

### Versioning

This document is versioned. Minor revisions (corrections, clarifications) increment the second digit. Material additions or changes (new tokens, new templates, voice rule changes) increment the first digit. Every version is dated, signed, and held in the asset library alongside its predecessors. Contractors are required to confirm the version they are working from at the start of each engagement.

### Review cycle

Reviewed every twelve months, or sooner if material change is required. The review is led by the owner and the brand custodian.

### Questions

Where this document is silent, the conviction decides. Where the conviction is silent, the question is escalated to the owner.
