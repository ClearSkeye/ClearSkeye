# Design System: Skeye Holding

## Overview

**Creative North Star: "The Clear Sky Ledger"**

This system should feel like a precise sky map for a portfolio, calm at first glance, then revealing clear structure and depth as users explore each company. The tone is premium and technical, but never cold, with restrained visual language that keeps attention on hierarchy and trust.

The visual atmosphere combines restrained color use with responsive motion. Color is intentional and sparse, used to guide action and focus rather than to decorate surfaces. Motion should provide confidence and orientation through clean state transitions, not spectacle.

This system explicitly rejects sterile corporate SaaS patterns, generic template hero sections, overused enterprise stock visual language, and corporate stock-photo enterprise look.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Structured, portfolio-first information hierarchy
- Restrained accents with high clarity neutrals
- Premium typographic rhythm over visual noise
- Responsive, purposeful interaction feedback

## Elevation

Depth should be mostly tonal and structural, with subtle lift only during interaction states. Since motion energy is responsive, elevation changes are state-driven and concise, never theatrical.

### Named Rules
**The State-Lift Rule.** Surfaces stay calm at rest. Any perceived lift appears as a direct response to hover, focus, or active state.

## Do's and Don'ts

Concrete guardrails for all future implementation passes.

### Do:
- **Do** keep palette usage restrained, with one sky-blue accent family and neutral-first layouts.
- **Do** use responsive transitions for state feedback, with clear focus treatment and predictable timing.
- **Do** maintain premium density through spacing rhythm and strong heading-to-body contrast.
- **Do** build trust with real portfolio structure and clear company relationships before decorative flourishes.

### Don't:
- **Don't** use sterile corporate SaaS patterns.
- **Don't** use generic template hero sections.
- **Don't** use overused enterprise stock visual language.
- **Don't** use corporate stock-photo enterprise look.
- **Don't** let accent color dominate large surface areas in restrained mode.
